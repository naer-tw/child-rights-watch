對應 CRC 條文：

兒童權利公約第二次國家報告條約專要文件【附件】

附件 7-28 (第 211 點)：受性傳播疾病影響的青少年人數

主題：兒少性傳染病感染人數增加，違反健康權

一、問題現況：

1. 我國 2016-2020 年受性傳播疾病影響的青少年人數先降後升，感染人數急遽增

加，15-24 歲年輕人淋病感染急劇增加，HIV(愛滋病毒)也依然有青少年感染，

梅毒數據更是遠高過歐美國家(見兒童權利公約第二次國家報告條約專要文件【附

件 7-28(第 211 點)：受性傳播疾病影響的青少年人數)】。

2

2. 台灣 2020 年有 314 位 15-24 歲年輕人感染 HIV，2021 年有 225 位 15-24 歲年輕

人感染 HIV，雖然愛滋感染人數已經下降，疾管署防疫有功，但 2021 年新增的

HIV(愛滋病毒)感染人數中有 83.71%是因為男男間的不安全性行為感染 (1,043

人)，政府應積極教育學生相關的健康知識,清楚性病與愛滋的感染風險 (附件

一)。

3. 根據 2000-2021 年的傳染病資料統計，附件二呈現出 15-24 歲 HIV 梅毒淋病感

染趨勢，青少年淋病感染率急遽上升 (附件二)。

4. 根據台灣、美國、歐盟 ECDC 的性傳染病資料統計，呈現出附件三 台歐美

HIV/梅毒/淋病比較表，台灣的淋病、HIV、梅毒的十萬人感染率比歐盟還高，

其中梅毒最為驚悚 (附件六)。

5. 查詢 2021 年全年度 HIV/淋病/梅毒 分齡統計，發現青少年罹患淋病、梅毒、

HIV 人數甚高 (附件三、四、五)， 10-19 歲青少年 814 人罹患淋病(男 645 / 女

169)，180 人罹患梅毒(男 150 / 女 30 )。

6. 根據蘋果日報 2022 年 3 月 22 日報導「媽媽嚇壞！15 歲男約砲後 GG 狂噴綠汁

代誌大條通報性侵」 ，員林基督教醫院泌尿科醫師陳昭安表示，有愈來愈多

九年級的學生患者，近 3 個月內，他已收治 5 名九年級淋病少男，年紀最小的

僅 15 歲，希望政府關注年輕族群淋病暴增的問題 (附件九之新聞報導)

3

二、 問題分析：

1. 性病防治教育內容不夠完整、未能與時俱進，誤導學生以為發生性行為和性傳

染病沒什麼大不了，輕忽自身健康權益。

(1) 學生反應學校的愛滋防治宣導缺乏愛滋病藥物副作用的教導，以及 PrEP 的

副作用且未能防範性傳染病；

(2) 亦有學生反應學校健康教育對於性傳染病的防治過於單一，僅強調保險套

功用，卻未能教導淋病、泡疹等均未能靠保險套做防範，也未教導學生在

青少年階段不發生性行為對於健康的好處、延後性行為發生的年紀對於健

康之好處。

2. 目前課本或宣導教學中並未充分告知學生相關風險:

LGBTQ 族群的健康風險較高、男男性行為健康風險較高(感染愛滋病毒的風險

性，肛交接受方是陰道交女方的 17.25 倍)，然而學校並未充分提出警告。 (附

件七、八)。

三、具體建議：

1. 除愛滋防治教育外，國中國小「課程課綱」還應請「衛生教育」專家增列「性傳染

病防治」能力指標和學習內容，教育學生台灣真實的現狀。

2. 性別平等教育議題課綱應邀請「健康教育」及「公共衛生」專家學者成為編審委

員，修訂課綱，增加愛滋及性傳染病有關的「能力指標項目」。

3. 健康與體育、健康與護理等教科書應加以改進:

(1) 分階段，適齡論述「性傳染病」對於年輕學子的健康之危害，包括性病、肝

炎、愛滋與罹患癌症之風險。

(2) 應說明肛門與陰道的生理構造差異，不同的感染風險也有很大的差異(附件

七)。應該明確告知延後性行為發生的年齡可大幅降低其感染性傳染病的機率，

以及穩定伴侶關係內的安全性行為對於性傳染病的預防效果。

(3) 在介紹保險套的功能時，應同時說明保險套的避孕和防範性傳染病之統計資料

僅針對「陰道性交」的部分，也應提供「男男性行為」感染性病統計數字。

4

(4) 論及兩性相處、青少年身體與親密關係探索的議題時，應同時放入情感教育的

議題內涵，讓年輕女性在探索生理層面之外，也能均衡發展提升其探索心理、

社會及心靈的滿足。並對網路社群交友的各種風險，做充分的說明。

4. 教師在教授性傳染病防治議題時，應兼顧學生的價值澄清與態度培養，幫助學生落

實性傳染病的防治。

5. 學校應規劃納入家長的力量、共同保護青少年免於性傳染病之危害，促使學校教育

成為社區公共事務的一環(退休校長王秀雲，2017)。

15 歲染淋病的少男，就診前已在坊間多家醫院就醫，但服藥治療不見改善。陳昭安問

淋病是第三類法定傳染病，國內去年新增 7380 例，好發年齡為 25-39 歲的青壯年人，

其中男多於女，病例數約為 13：1。

淋病患者下體會流出分泌物，因此患者內褲常是黃一塊、綠一塊的。資料照片

陳昭安表示，淋病的致病菌是奈瑟氏淋病雙球菌，男、女性感染後症狀不太一樣，男

21

性患者可能症狀有尿道化膿，排尿有刺痛或灼熱感，有些病患會自行痊癒，但有些病

而且灼痛的症狀，約有 20％之病患因月經週期導致子宮感染，引起子宮內膜炎、輸卵

管炎或骨盤腔腹膜炎，最後甚至造成子宮外孕或不孕症。

此外，新生兒生產經過產道時，可能因為媽媽淋病感染，使新生兒的眼睛感染淋菌性

醫師表示，性行為是淋病最主要的傳播方式，另與感染者的黏膜滲出物接觸也可能感

染，因此不論是性行為或口交，都要戴上保險套或口交套來防護。

Article of Treaty or Act involved :

Appendix 7-28(Article 211) of the Convention-specific

Document

Theme:

Rising number of children and adolescents are infected with sexually

transmitted diseases (STDs) is a violation of the right to health.

I. Status of the problem

1. From 2016 to 2020, the number of adolescents affected by sexually

transmitted diseases (STDs) in Taiwan first decreased and then increased,

and the number of infected cases soared sharply. The number of youngsters

infected with gonorrhea rose swiftly among the age group of 15 to 24. HIV

infection was still observed among adolescents, and numbers related to

syphilis was far higher than that of European countries and the United States

(please refer to Convention on the Rights of the Child: Second National

Report Treaty-Specific Document [Annex7-28 (point 211): number of

adolescents affected by STDs])

2

2. In Taiwan, 314 youngsters aged 15 to 24 was infected with HIV in 2020, and

225 youngsters aged 15 to 24 was infected with HIV in 2021. Although the

number of HIV infections declined, thanks to the prevention measures of

Taiwan Centers for Disease Control (CDC), 83.71% of the new HIV

infection cases in 2021 (1,043 people) were caused by unsafe sexual contacts

actively educate students about related health knowledge so that they are

clearly aware of the risks of STDs and AIDS infection (Annex 1).

II. Analysis of the problem

1. Education on the prevention of STDs has not been sufficiently

comprehensive and relevant, misleading students into believing that sexual

contacts and STDs are no big deals, thus overlooking their own health

benefits.

(1) Students’ feedbacks reflected that AIDS prevention campaigns at

schools were in lack of information related to the side effects of AIDS

drugs, as well as the side effects of pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) and

how it could not prevent STDs.

(2) In addition, some students revealed that the health education at school

was too simplistic in the prevention and treatment of STDs. It only

emphasized the function of condoms, but failed to caution that gonorrhea

and herpes could not be prevented by condoms, nor did it show the health

benefits of not having sex during teenage years and of having sex at an

older age.

5

2. The current textbooks or educational campaigns did not sufficiently

inform students about the relevant risks.

The LGBTQ group and MSM have higher health risks (risk of receivers

of anal sex being infected with HIV is 17.25 times higher than that of

female partner of vaginal sex), but schools failed to sufficiently caution

students about it (Annex 7 & 8).

III. Specific suggestions

1. In addition to AIDS prevention education, the curriculum syllabus of

elementary schools and junior high schools should be added with capacity

indicators and learning materials of "prevention of STDs” by health

education experts to show the real situation of Taiwan to students.

2. Experts and scholars in health education and public health should be

invited to serve as reviewers of the syllabus of gender equality

education to edit the syllabus and add capacity indicators related to

AIDS and STDs.

3. Textbooks on health and physical education, and health and nursing

should be improved in the following areas:

(1) The content should be divided into stages and provide explanation,

6

appropriate for different age groups of young students, on the health

hazards of STDs, including diseases related to sexual contacts, hepatitis,

(2) Explanation of the differences in physiology of the anus and vagina

should be provided, as well as the differences in infection risks (Annex

7). Information such as having sex at an older age can significantly

reduce the chance of being infected by STDs, and the preventive

effect of safe sex in a stable partnership on STDs should be explicitly

provided.

(3) When introducing the function of condoms, it should also be stated that

the statistics on contraception and prevention of STDs of condoms are

only applicable to vaginal sex. Statistics on STDs among MSM should

also be provided.

(4) With teenagers, when discussing issues related to getting along with the

other sex, or exploring one’s own body and close relationships,

emotional aspects of these issues should be incorporated as well, so that

young females can develop a balanced knowledge of psychological,

social and mental satisfaction while exploring their body. A

comprehensive explanation on the risks of making friends online or on

7

social media platforms should also be provided.

4. When teaching the prevention and treatment of STDs, teachers should also

set students’ values straight and cultivate an appropriate attitude so to help

them put the prevention and treatment of STDs into practice.

5. Schools should plan to incorporate the strength of parents to jointly protect

young people from STDs, so that school education becomes a part of public

affairs of the community (retired principal Wang Hsiu-Yun, 2017).
