2. International Evidence

CRPD §5 (equality and non-discrimination) + §6 (women with disabilities)

§5 mandates elimination of disability-based multiple discrimination, including SOGIESC.

§6's multiple-discrimination framework extends to disabled LGBTQI+.

CRPD §25 (health) + §17 (bodily integrity)

Health rights must be accessible without discrimination. Disabled LGBTQI+ persons additionally face:

  • Mental-health-service homophobia / transphobia
  • Intellectually disabled persons' SOGIESC being "infantilized" or denied
  • Conversion-therapy risk

Yogyakarta Principles plus 10 (2017)

First international soft-law document recognizing the disability + SOGIESC intersection.

International benchmarks

  • NIVEL Netherlands (2019): Disabled LGB persons' mental-health problems are 2.3× higher than disabled non-LGB
  • EU FRA (2020): Transgender disabled persons face 63% employment discrimination (vs 38% general transgender)
